TURN COAT

What is a Turncoat? A turncoat is a form of debating in which a single speaker has to speak both for and against a given topic, switching sides upon the coordinator's cue. The main component of a turncoat event is to maintain coherence in an argument even when switching.

Event Rules

  • Parrticipation: This is an individual event.
  • Repetition of points and paper reading are prohbited.
  • Topics for the first round will be announced 24 hours before the event. Topics for the successive rounds will be relased on-spot with appropriate preparation time.
  • Each participant gets 4 minutes for the speech, exceeding this limit will lead to a point deduction.
  • Participants must rely solely on provided background knowledge, internet usage is prohibited after the debate begins.
  • The decisions of judges will be final and no arguments or appeal will be entertained.
